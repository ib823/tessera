## The Sheraton Move was elite payoff, not ideology

The Sheraton Move (Langkah Sheraton) of February 2020 was not primarily about ideology, "Malay unity," or national salvation — the public framing pushed by participants. It was a classic elite realignment driven by personal ambition, patronage payoffs, and immediate material rewards. A small group of operators traded parliamentary support for high office, GLC control, and influence.

## Core personal incentives of key actors

### Muhyiddin Yassin (Bersatu president, then-Home Affairs Minister)
- **Primary payoff:** The Prime Ministership itself. In the PH government under Mahathir, Muhyiddin was senior but subordinate. The move elevated him to the eighth Prime Minister on 1 March 2020, heading Perikatan Nasional.
- **Secondary benefits:** Control over cabinet formation and patronage networks. He could distribute posts to loyalists without accommodating Anwar Ibrahim or the multi-ethnic PH structure.
- **Motivation pattern:** Long-standing rivalry with Mahathir + desire to lead a Malay-centric coalition. He had been sidelined once before (resigned as DPM in 2015 under Najib).

### Azmin Ali (PKR deputy president, then-Economic Affairs Minister)
- **Primary payoff:** Escape from Anwar Ibrahim's shadow + elevation to de facto number-two status. Azmin led the faction of ~11 PKR MPs who defected. He was sacked from PKR on 24 February 2020, joined Bersatu, and was immediately appointed one of four Senior Ministers (explicitly the "most senior") with oversight of financial/economic affairs and later MITI.
- **Additional perks:** Access to GLC boards and policy influence.
- **Motivation:** Open rivalry with Anwar over the premiership transition timeline.

### The ~11 PKR defectors (Azmin faction) + Bersatu bloc
- **Immediate rewards:** Cabinet and deputy-ministerial posts, GLC appointments, and titles. Switchers were "rewarded with cabinet positions, government-linked appointments, honorific titles and other opportunities."
- **Examples:** Zuraida Kamaruddin (Housing Minister), Saifuddin Abdullah, Kamaruddin Jaffar, Rashid Hasnon and others received deputy ministerships or equivalent patronage.
- **Systematic GLC capture:** PN MPs were placed on boards of SOCSO, FELCRA, MPOB, and regional development authorities.

## Why personal incentives dominated structural narratives

The dossier correctly flags that the Sheraton Move is often framed as "structural" (coalition fractures, Article 40 discretion, anti-hopping loopholes). The harder truth is that it was personal horse-trading: defectors extracted upfront private payoffs (posts, contracts, immunity from intra-party purges) in exchange for flipping the government. Public justifications ("saving the country from PH infighting" or "Malay dignity") were post-hoc rationalisations.

**Outcome reality check:** The PN government lasted ~17 months before collapsing under its own internal pivots (2021). Recent PKR lawsuits (2026) demanding RM10 million bonds from some defectors underscore how raw the betrayal felt to the original 2018 mandate.
