# Brief — e-invoicing exemption and the shadow-economy gap

## ISSUE
Malaysia's e-invoicing mandate (MyInvois, run by LHDN) was sold as the lever to narrow the shadow economy and tax gap. Between 6 December 2025 and 5 January 2026 the government walked the mandate back twice — first raising the exemption threshold to RM1 million turnover, then postponing compliance for RM1–5 million firms by another year. SAMENTA welcomed the relief and said 200,000 more SMEs would be spared. The same segment is where most cash-economy under-reporting is documented.

## PERIOD
August 2024 (Phase 1 go-live) through May 2026 (current).

## CONTEXT — TIMELINE
- **1 August 2024**: Phase 1 enforcement begins for firms with turnover >RM100M (LHDN implementation timeline).
- **1 January 2025**: Phase 2 begins for RM25M–RM100M.
- **1 July 2025**: Phase 3 begins for RM5M–RM25M.
- **July 2025**: LHDN says 5,800 individuals flagged for failing to declare RM484M income; cases include online operators, commission earners, and luxury-car buyers (TheVibes/LHDN statement, July 2025).
- **November 2025**: LHDN raids company linked to luxury car cartel — alleges roughly RM70M in undeclared sales (Malay Mail/FMT, 18 Nov 2025).
- **6 December 2025**: PM Anwar Ibrahim, speaking at "Sentuhan Madani Bersama Rakyat Sabah," announces e-invoicing exemption threshold rises from RM500,000 to RM1M turnover; tax refund allocation doubled from RM2B to RM4B (Borneo Post; The Star; Business Today).
- **7 December 2025**: SAMENTA welcomes the move, says 200,000 additional SMEs will be spared (Bernama; Malay Mail; The Star; FMT).
- **22 December 2025**: LHDN CEO Datuk Dr Abu Tariq Jamaluddin says 820.5M e-invoices issued from 111,600 taxpayers; Phase 1-2 compliance >90%; Phase 3 at 80% (The Star; The Vibes).
- **1 January 2026**: RM1M exemption takes effect; original Phase 5 (RM150K–RM500K) effectively cancelled (LHDN; Comarch; VATupdate).
- **5 January 2026**: PM Anwar announces e-invoicing for RM1M–RM5M firms postponed another year (FMT; The Star; The Edge).

## ACTORS
- **Anwar Ibrahim** — Prime Minister; made both relaxation announcements.
- **Datuk Dr Abu Tariq Jamaluddin** — LHDN CEO; reports compliance and volume figures.
- **SAMENTA** (Small and Medium Enterprises Association of Malaysia) — industry voice welcoming relief; cited 200,000-SME figure.
- **MDEC, MOF, BSN, MCMC** — joint administrators of MSME Digital Grant (RM5,000 50% matching, RM50M budget for 2025).
- **MyInvois** — LHDN's e-invoice clearance platform (CTC model with 55 fields, JSON/XML formats).

## RELEVANT LAW / POLICY
- **Income Tax Act 1967 ss. 82A, 113B** — basis for mandatory e-invoicing under LHDN's delegated rule-making.
- **MyInvois Guideline** (LHDN, updated December 2025) — technical and procedural specifications, including 55 mandatory fields and CTC clearance flow.
- **MSME Digital Grant MADANI** (Budget 2025) — RM50M, 50% matching grant up to RM5,000 per SME for digital tool adoption including ERP/accounting/e-invoicing.

- **820.5M e-invoices issued from 111,600 taxpayers** as of 22 Dec 2025 — LHDN CEO statement (The Star, 22 Dec 2025; The Vibes).
- **Phase 1-2 compliance >90%, Phase 3 at 80%** — LHDN CEO, same statement.

## CONTRADICTIONS
- **Shadow economy size**: Sources put it at 21% (Focus Malaysia summary), 24.2% (IJBS averaged research), and 25.3% (World Economics). I used 21-25% range in the brief; the 21%/RM330B is the most conservative widely-cited point estimate and was used in card big text. The range is acknowledged in the sub.
- **Tax gap losses**: Estimates vary from RM70B (LoanStreet citing aggregate analyst work) to RM117B (BNM-cited 24.2% × GDP). I used ">RM70B" as a defensible floor. The discrepancy is methodological — BNM's figure projects shadow-economy share against GDP; analyst figures look at actual tax revenue gap.
- **Date of "more than RM100M turnover" first wave**: LHDN said RM100M+; some early reporting said "RM150M turnover threshold for first wave" before later corrections. I used the LHDN-confirmed RM100M.
