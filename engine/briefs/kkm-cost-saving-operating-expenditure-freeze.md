## ISSUE

On 28 April 2026 a Treasury directive proposed RM10 billion in across-the-board spending cuts for the financial year, triggered by the West Asia (Iran) conflict pushing Malaysia's 2026 subsidy bill from the RM15 billion budgeted toward a projected RM58.4 billion. The Health Ministry's (KKM) share is a proposed **RM3.06 billion cut** (6.6%) to its **RM46.5 billion** 2026 allocation — which would leave it RM43.4 billion, *below* its RM45.3 billion 2025 budget. KKM's public response was that it would find "cost savings without affecting essential services" and submit a counter-proposal. The T4A finding: roughly **62% of KKM's operating budget is staff salaries** (emoluments, politically and operationally fixed), and the minister has ring-fenced core clinical services as well. The cut therefore has to fall on a residual base far smaller than the cut itself — and clinicians say the "non-essential" items (cleaning, maintenance, supplies) are what keep wards safe. "Without affecting essential services" is arithmetically strained, not reassuring.

## RELEVANT FRAMEWORK

- No specific statute; this is executive fiscal management under the **Federal Constitution Art. 96–104** (federal consolidated fund, supply, expenditure) and the annual Supply Act. A mid-year operating-expenditure realignment is an administrative Treasury action, not a fresh parliamentary appropriation — which is itself part of the accountability gap (cuts to an already-passed Supply Act allocation, without a fresh vote).
- **Fiscal Responsibility Act 2023** — the medium-term fiscal framework context driving deficit discipline.

**The arithmetic of the reframe:** RM39.8b opex − RM24.8b emoluments (protected) = ~RM15b non-salary opex. Strip protected clinical supplies/medicine from that, and a RM3.06b (or RM4.65b at 10%) cut must land on a base of roughly RM13.9b services & supplies + development. That is why clinicians argue "non-essential" is a near-empty category: at this depth the cut reaches cleaning, maintenance, utilities — which are infection-control and equipment-uptime, i.e. patient safety.

## DRAFT EDITORIAL DIRECTION (for Stage 1)

- **Hook (what they said):** Government says it can cut billions from the health budget "without affecting essential services." Plant the gap: 62% of that budget is salaries it cannot touch.
- **Fact 1 (Governance, identifiable lead):** The Treasury proposed a RM3.06 billion cut (6.6%) — leaving KKM at RM43.4b, below its 2025 budget.
- **Fact 2 (Economic, the shocking number — arousal peak):** RM24.8 billion (62%) of KKM's RM39.8b operating budget is staff salaries, which a cut cannot realistically reach. The cut must fall on the ~RM14b of services and supplies.
- **Fact 3 (Health, the missing voice):** Clinicians say that residual base *is* essential — cleaning prevents infection, maintenance keeps equipment alive, supplies are medicine, gloves, oxygen.
- **Reframe (question restructuring):** The question is not whether the cut spares "essential services." It is whether, after salaries and core care are ring-fenced, "non-essential" is a real category or an accounting fiction.
- **Analogy (optional):** Like told to cut a household's spending without touching rent or food — what's left is the lightbulbs, and you're told the dark won't matter.
- **View (WhatsApp-quotable):** A budget that is 62% salaries cannot be trimmed by billions without the cut landing where patients feel it; the honest debate is which harm to accept, not whether harm occurs.
