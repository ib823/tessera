# Brief — ASEAN e-invoice alternatives (sibling to 1981)

## ISSUE
Malaysia raised its e-invoicing exemption to RM1m turnover on 6 December 2025 and extended the RM1m-5m phase first to end-2026 (5 Jan 2026) then to end-2027 (20 Apr 2026). India, Singapore, Indonesia, and Thailand chose different paths to bring SMEs into the digital tax trail — higher thresholds, direct subsidies, simplified compliance lanes, or graduated technical tiers. Each path trades visibility against cost burden differently. Malaysia's binary exempt/full-clearance model is one option among several, not the only one.

## PERIOD
Comparative — Indian rule effective 1 Aug 2023; Singapore rollout 2025-2031; Indonesia regime current; Thailand regime current.

## ACTORS
- **LHDN (Malaysia)** — administers MyInvois clearance with 55 mandatory fields in JSON/XML.
- **CBIC (India)** — issued Notification 10/2023 setting the ₹5 crore threshold from 1 Aug 2023.
- **IRAS (Singapore)** — administers GST InvoiceNow rollout; offers SME grants.
- **IMDA (Singapore)** — joint announcement on InvoiceNow rollout extension (Committee of Supply 2026).
- **DGT/DJP (Indonesia)** — administers e-Faktur and UMKM final-tax regime.
- **Revenue Department (Thailand)** — administers both XML and email-route e-Tax invoicing.

## KEY STATISTICS — with sources

- **India**: e-invoicing mandatory for taxpayers with annual aggregate turnover over ₹5 crore (~RM2.8m at current rates), effective 1 August 2023. Source: CBIC Notification No. 10/2023 – Central Tax dated 10 May 2023; multiple Indian tax-press confirmations.

- **Singapore**: GST InvoiceNow rollout begins 1 Nov 2025 for newly incorporated voluntary GST registrants; expands 1 Apr 2026 to all new voluntary registrants; progressive rollout April 2028 to April 2031 for all existing GST-registered businesses. SME grant: up to S$1,000 per SME for InvoiceNow-Ready Solutions; additional up to S$5,000 grant for early adopters; free-of-charge ("FOC") solution packages available until 31 March 2027. Source: IRAS GST InvoiceNow Requirement page (PRIMARY); IRAS Committee of Supply 2026 announcement.

- **Indonesia**: VAT registration (PKP) and e-Faktur obligation triggered at IDR 4.8 billion (~RM1.36m at current rates) annual gross turnover. Below this, registration is optional. UMKM (small-business) regime offers a 0.5% final tax on gross turnover up to IDR 4.8 billion. Source: DGT/MoF-164; PwC Indonesia Pocket Tax Book 2025; Acclime Indonesia.

- **Thailand**: Two parallel e-Tax invoice routes. (1) Full XML with digital signature for any registered taxpayer. (2) "e-Tax Invoice by Email" with timestamp verification for entities with annual turnover under THB 30 million (~RM3.8m at current rates) — PDF/A-3 format. Approved issuers transmit monthly e-document data to RD by the 15th of the following month. Source: Thailand Revenue Department guidelines; Sovos; multiple tax-advisory primaries.

- **Malaysia (for comparison)**: Exempt under RM1m turnover (from 1 Jan 2026); full LHDN MyInvois clearance above, with 55 mandatory fields in JSON/XML. MSME Digital Grant offers 50% match up to RM5,000 per company; tax deductions on e-invoicing costs and ICT/consulting. Source: LHDN e-invoice timeline; MDEC SME Digitalisation Grant page; Budget 2025 documents.

## CONTRADICTIONS / Stage 4 corrections applied

The parent issue 1981's Stage 4 reviewer cited two figures that did not survive Stage 6 self-verification:
- "Singapore free InvoiceNow-Ready solutions until March 2031" — IRAS primary source confirms the FOC packages are available until **31 March 2027**. The 2031 figure refers to the progressive rollout end-date for all existing GST-registered businesses, not the FOC window.
- "Indonesia exempts e-commerce sellers below IDR 5 billion" — the canonical VAT/e-Faktur threshold is **IDR 4.8 billion** (PMK/MoF-164). The IDR 500 million figure Stage 4 implicitly used belongs to a separate Article 22 WHT exemption for individual e-commerce sellers.

Both corrected in this brief and in the published cards.
