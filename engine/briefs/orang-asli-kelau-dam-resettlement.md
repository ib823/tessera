# Issue 1581 — Orang Asli Kelau Dam Resettlement and Clean Water

## CONTEXT

Kelau Dam is part of the Pahang–Selangor Raw Water Transfer Project (ISRWT), built to supply Selangor/Klang Valley. The project displaced Orang Asli Temuan communities in Bentong/Raub area to Sungai Bilut resettlement. Resettlement and post-displacement living conditions have been challenged by Orang Asli themselves and by Malaysian civil society.

---

## VERIFIED CLAIMS (against primary or near-primary source)

1. **Kelau Dam is on Sungai Kelau in Raub, Pahang**, 30m high, 24km² area, 147.1 million m³ storage capacity. Source: Wikipedia Bahasa Melayu citing project documents; FoE Japan project description (https://www.foejapan.org/en/aid/jbic02/kelau/index.html).

2. **Designed daily transfer: 1,890 million litres of raw water** (i.e. 1.89 billion L/day) to Langat 2 plant via 44.6km tunnel. Source: FoE Japan project page; project design documents cited via Wikipedia.

3. **Approximately 13,000 Orang Asli Temuan in Bentong were affected** by the project. Source: FoE Japan; Intercontinental Cry "Drowned Forests and Damned Lives" (https://intercontinentalcry.org/drowned-forests-and-damned-lives/).

4. **27 Orang Asli filed judicial review of the EIA in October 2007; High Court dismissed in 2010.** Source: Malaysiakini ("Kelau: Permohonan orang Asli ditolak", https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/141273).

5. **Resettlement to Sungai Bilut on 800 acres.** Each household received: 3-bedroom bungalow, 6 acres individual land, 5 acres palm oil cultivation, 6 acres fruit trees, and monthly allowance of RM638. Source: Intercontinental Cry; FoE Japan; corroborated in Cultural Survival writeup.

6. **Documented water-access complaints post-resettlement.** Aliran ("Lost forever: Free-flowing river that gave the Temuans 24-hour access to water"); World Rainforest Movement reports women's accounts of post-resettlement water rationing and the loss of the free-flowing Sungai Selangor source.

## EDITORIAL FINDING

The structural argument is anchored in genuine, well-documented phenomena (Orang Asli displacement, post-resettlement water-access loss). But every load-bearing specific number in the current 1581 cards is either unverified or contradicts a primary source. The "1.1 billion litres daily" claim contradicts the documented 1.89 billion L/day design transfer.

### Recommendation: Hold (unpublish) and rewrite

This is the same pattern as 1170. The salvageable framing would use the verified numbers (13,000 affected, 27 plaintiffs, 1.89B L/day transfer design, Sungai Bilut resettlement terms incl. RM638/month allowance) and drop the specific RM82M/RM31M/RM8M/RM0 audit-breakdown claim and the 340-family / 4x-illness specifics.

Until rewritten, the issue should not auto-publish via fallback rotation.
