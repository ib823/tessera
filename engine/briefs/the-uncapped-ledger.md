## THESIS
Malaysia's fiscal debate anchors on one reassuring number (debt ~64% of GDP, "under the 65% ceiling"),
while the exposures that carry no statutory ceiling (total liabilities 84.1%, RM424.7B guarantees, the
trillion-ringgit pension obligation, a thinning tax base) compound out of view. The core claims rest on
reported actuals; the debt-path and pension trajectories are T4A extrapolations, labelled as such.

## KEY FIGURES (with status)
- Statutory debt 63.5% / total federal 64.7% / debt+liabilities 84.1% of GDP, end-June 2025 [ACTUAL, MOF].
  Only statutory is bound by the 65% ceiling. [primary PDF 403; corroborated by The Star, The Edge]
- Deficit 4.1% (2024 ACTUAL), 3.8%/3.5% (2025/26 targets); FRA Act 850 benchmark 3% deficit / 60% debt,
  both missed; 3% goal now ~2028 [MOF; IMF Art IV 2026].
- Guarantees RM424.7B = 21.1% GDP (cap 25%); PPP RM112B; stress test ~97% GDP if all called [MOF].
- Pension spend RM26.39B (2020) -> RM32.01B (2023); pensioners nearing 1M [Dewan Rakyat]. CAGR 8.6%.
  Official projection RM46.4B (2030), RM120B (2040); T4A log-linear fit RM50.8B (2030), RM98B (2040, PI 79-121).
  [actuals verifiable; projections are extrapolations]
- Old-age support ratio ~7.9 (2022) -> ~4.6 (2040) working-age per elderly [DOSM official projection].
- RON95/BUDI95 savings target revised RM8B -> RM2.5-4B; World Bank disputes [MOF; The Edge].

## METHOD (figures fig-1..fig-4)
Debt path uses the standard debt-dynamics recursion (this year = last year scaled by interest and growth,
plus primary deficit), r=4.2% (debt service / debt stock), two scenarios (g=6% consolidation -> 58.5% by
2035; g=4.5% stalled -> 73.7%). Pension uses log-linear OLS + 8.6% CAGR, bracketing the official
projection. All charts are T4A-rendered (white-on-navy, accent #9B2C2C) via matplotlib.
