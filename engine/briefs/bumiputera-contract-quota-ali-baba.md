## CONTEXT

Malaysia reserves portions of government procurement for Bumiputera firms (ethnic Malays and Borneo indigenous peoples) under the New Economic Policy (1971) and successors. "Ali Baba" arrangements — where a Bumiputera firm wins a reserved contract and subcontracts the actual work to non-Bumiputera operators for a commission — are a long-documented form of rent extraction that diverts the policy's intended redistributive benefit. MACC, MOF, and civil society have flagged this repeatedly. In 2025 the Government Procurement Act introduced new transparency mechanisms.

## CONTRADICTIONS

Where reputable sources differ on the same fact:

1. **Total annual cost of Ali Baba vs total corruption:** MACC publicly quantifies *all corruption* at RM277B over 5 years (~RM55B/year). Aliran ("How we lose hundreds of billions to middlemen") frames the cost as "hundreds of billions" cumulatively, not annually. No source isolates Ali Baba specifically at RM12B/year.

2. **Quota share:** Some sources describe "up to 30%" as a ceiling (MOF/CPTPP framing); others use "30%" as if it were a floor. Both are heard in policy commentary.
