# ISSUE 1990 — Rafizi's RM1.1B Arm Deal: Whose Process Failed, the Minister's or the Probe's?

## ISSUE
A flagship semiconductor partnership signed by Malaysia's Ministry of
Economy with Britain's Arm Holdings is now the subject of a MACC criminal
probe. The MACC has recommended charges against two unnamed individuals;
the former Economy Minister who signed it is voluntarily handing in
additional evidence. The same government is pressing on with the deal
while the agency moves to charge the people who designed it. The
substantive question is not Rafizi's character — it is whether the
**procurement procedure** for a 10-year, RM1.1 billion IP-licensing
arrangement was lawful, and whether the **timing and method** of the
agency's announcement was institutionally proper.

## PERIOD
March 2025 (deal signing) — May 2026 (probe in final stages; AGC
charging decision pending).

## CONTEXT (Timeline)

- **Feb 28, 2025**: PM Anwar publicly confirms Arm Holdings agreement to
  be signed the following week. Malay Mail, "Malaysia secures UK's ARM
  Holdings investment".
- **Mar 5, 2025**: Strategic partnership signed in Kuala Lumpur. PM Anwar
  + Arm CEO Rene Haas. Signatories: MIDA + Arm Limited. Rafizi Ramli
  (then Economy Minister) the lead ministerial figure. Deal value: US$250M
  over 10 years (≈ RM1.11B). Deliverables: 7 chip design blueprints
  ("CSS tokens" + Flexible Access), training pipeline of 10,000
  engineers, Arm's first ASEAN office in KL. Anwar framed it as "the
  start of Malaysia's second semiconductor wave."
- **May 23, 2025**: Rafizi loses PKR deputy-president election to Nurul
  Izzah Anwar (9,803 to 3,866 votes). The Star.
- **May 28, 2025**: Rafizi submits resignation as Economy Minister.
  Effective 17 Jun 2025. He stated his defeat means he "no longer has
  the party's mandate."
- **Feb 11, 2026**: MACC deputy chief commissioner (operations) Datuk
  Seri Ahmad Khusairi Yahaya confirms complaints from three NGOs
  alleging the Arm agreement was "rushed and one-sided" exposing the
  government to financial risk. Review begins.
- **Feb 16, 2026**: MACC opens investigation paper. Sources differ
  between Feb 16 and Mar 4 on the *formal* opening date — Feb 16 is
  the internal paper, Mar 4 is the first formal public summons round.
- **Feb 23, 2026**: New Economy Minister Datuk Akmal Nasrullah confirms
  Putrajaya will press on with the Arm deal despite the probe, citing
  "the country's image" and the 10-year structural commitment. Bernama.
- **Mar 4, 2026**: MACC issues a public search notice for James Chai
  (Chai Jin Shern), Rafizi's former Special Functions Officer who works
  in the UK. The notice published his childhood-home address; he calls
  it "excessive and potentially defamatory." MACC says it had tried to
  reach him by WhatsApp since 24 Feb, that he blocked the officer on
  26 Feb, and the public notice followed.
- **Mar 4, 2026**: Chai publicly states "I am not Jho Low 2.0,"
  pre-empting an online narrative comparing him to the 1MDB fugitive.
- **Mar 6, 2026**: Chai appoints lawyers, pledges full cooperation.
- **Apr 17, 2026**: MACC announces statement-taking from Chai
  scheduled for 28 Apr.
- **Apr 28, 2026**: Chai gives statement at MACC HQ.
- **May 1, 2026**: Rafizi publicly confirms he has been summoned by MACC
  for Monday 4 May, says he "expects possible charges."
- **May 4 – May 7, 2026**: Rafizi gives statements over four days;
  131 pages of statement recorded. Investigation focused on
  decision-making process, procedures, meetings, documents, minutes.
- **May 6, 2026**: MACC chief commissioner Tan Sri Azam Baki announces
  the agency has recommended charges against two individuals (unnamed)
  under MACC Act 2009 Section 23 (abuse of power), Penal Code Section
  420 (cheating), Penal Code Section 409 (criminal breach of trust).
  22 witnesses recorded; two more (one in Taiwan, one in Malaysia)
  remain. Final charging decision rests with AGC.
- **May 11, 2026**: Three Malaysian firms — SkyeChip, Oppstar, GreatAsic
  — named as first to access Arm's design technology. The Star /
  TheEdge.
- **May 12, 2026**: Azam Baki's tenure as MACC chief ends, six days
  after his charging-recommendation announcement.
- **May 14, 2026**: Rafizi returns to MACC HQ voluntarily with 28
  additional pages of documentary evidence plus a USB drive of
  documents and video recordings (which the MACC confirmed receiving).
  Rafizi's stated frame: the deal was "widely reported by the media with
  statements and explanations from senior national leaders" — i.e., not
  conducted in secret like 1MDB. He completes the session in two hours,
  says it "went smoothly."

## RELEVANT LAW

- **MACC Act 2009, Section 23** — Offence of using office or position
  for gratification ("abuse of power"). Maximum penalty 20 years +
  fine of 5× the gratification value.
- **Penal Code Section 420** — Cheating and dishonestly inducing
  delivery of property. Up to 10 years' imprisonment + whipping + fine.
- **Penal Code Section 409** — Criminal breach of trust by public
  servant or agent. Up to 20 years' imprisonment + whipping + fine.
- **Federal Constitution Article 145** — Attorney-General's
  prosecutorial discretion: AGC decides whether MACC's recommendation
  becomes a charge.
- **Government Procurement Treasury Instructions / MOF e-Perolehan
  rules** — Procedural requirements for government contract-without-
  tender and strategic-partnership instruments. The procurement track
  used for the Arm deal sits at the seam between an IP-licensing
  arrangement, a strategic partnership, and a public-spend commitment;
  the procedural question turns on which category governs.
- **Government Procurement Act 2023** (in force) — Imposes higher
  transparency standards on large-value contracts.

## 12-DIMENSION RISK ASSESSMENT

1. **Sentiment** — HIGH: Story carries 1MDB-comparison weight whether or
   not the comparison is warranted; emotional load is high on all sides.
2. **Political** — CRITICAL: Probe targets a former minister of the
   ruling coalition's largest party (PKR) by a MACC whose own chief is
   contested. Reads as either reform or factional payback depending on
   prior.
3. **Ethnic** — LOW: Cross-cuts; not an ethnic narrative on its face.
4. **Religious** — LOW: Not a religious matter.
5. **Narrative** — CRITICAL: "Reform vs witch-hunt" framing dominates.
   T4A must avoid both poles and centre the **procedural** question.
6. **Completeness** — HIGH: AGC has not charged; speculation outpaces
   evidence. The two recommended persons are unnamed. The exact
   procurement failing alleged has not been publicly itemised.
7. **Temporal** — MEDIUM: Probe in final stages but no charging
   decision; further events (AGC decision, possible court filings)
   will reshape the picture within weeks.
8. **Confidence** — MEDIUM: Multiple credible outlets agree on the
   factual spine; one set of tech outlets (Tom's Hardware, Yahoo
   tech-wire, startupnews) conflated the Arm probe with a separate
   January 2026 ex-army-chief defence-procurement probe — that
   conflation is wrong and must be flagged in cards if any number
   from that line surfaces.
9. **Sources** — MEDIUM-HIGH: National outlets (Bernama, NST, Malay
   Mail, The Star, FMT, Edge, TheVibes), Reuters, AFP, Bloomberg, SCMP;
   some access via paywall/Cloudflare. MIDA press release on signing.
10. **Geographic** — LOW: Putrajaya / KL centric.
11. **Economic** — HIGH: Deal's RM7–9.4B annual-revenue target depends
    on continued credibility of the IP-access pipeline; a stalled probe
    could chill the 25+7 token allocation programme even if the deal
    text is upheld.
12. **Gender** — LOW: Not a gendered story.

## CONTRADICTIONS

- **Formal-investigation-opening date**: One stream of reporting (e.g.
  SCMP, AFP) gives **4 March 2026** as the date MACC formally launched
  the investigation and summoned 12 individuals. Another stream (e.g.
  the Vibes, Malay Mail) cites **16 February 2026** as the date the
  investigation paper was opened, with the public-summons round
  following on 4 March. T4A position: treat 16 Feb 2026 as the internal
  paper-opening and 4 March 2026 as the formal first summons round.
  Cite the *Feb 11* announcement of complaint receipt by the deputy
  commissioner; cite *Mar 4* for the public search notice; avoid
  collapsing the two.

- **Identity of the two recommended for charges**: Azam Baki did not
  name them. Speculation centres on Rafizi + James Chai. Until AGC
  files, do not state in cards that they are the two — frame as
  "widely speculated" or simply describe the public actions of each
  without conflating speculation with the recommendation.

- **Total deal value, USD vs MYR**: US$250M ≈ RM1.11B at 2025 rates.
  Some outlets round to RM1.1B, others to RM1.11B. Both are correct.
  Use **RM1.1 billion** as the canonical T4A figure (matches MACC's
  public framing).

- **Army-chief / safehouse seizure**: Tom's Hardware, startupnews.fyi,
  and Yahoo Finance bundled a Jan 2026 ex-army-chief defence-
  procurement seizure (RM11.4M assets, "two wives and associates," per
  Star + OCCRP + Nikkei Asia) into headlines about the Arm probe. The
  two are **separate cases**. Do not cite the RM11.4M / $1.27M safehouse
  / former-army-chief detail in any Arm-issue card. If a Stage 2/3
  reviewer flags this, point to this CONTRADICTIONS note.

- **Investigation status — "near completion" vs "additional evidence
  still incoming"**: Azam's 6 May statement said the probe was in its
  final stages with two witnesses outstanding. Rafizi's 14 May submission
  of fresh material does not retract that — MACC accepted the
  evidence for the probe paper that goes to AGC, not as a reopening.
  Cards should not imply the submission stalls the prosecution.

## EDITORIAL FRAME (provisional, for Stage 1)

T4A's distinct contribution: the **procedural question**.

Mainstream framing is binary: Rafizi-is-Jho-Low or Rafizi-is-being-
witch-hunted. Both poles miss what is actually testable in court.

Three procedural questions the cards should help readers ask:
1. **What procurement track did the deal use?** Strategic partnership,
   IP licence, MoU-with-payment, or contract-without-tender. The legal
   exposure on each is different.
2. **What were the selection criteria for the 3 + 22 + 7 token
   recipients?** Were they published? Was there a competitive process?
3. **What is the institutional propriety of an outgoing MACC chief
   announcing a charging recommendation six days before tenure ends?**
   This is the structural question regardless of Rafizi's guilt.

The reframe should rotate readers from "did Rafizi do it" to "is the
procurement architecture and the enforcement architecture *both* fit
for handling deals of this size and shape." Same facts, structural
question.
