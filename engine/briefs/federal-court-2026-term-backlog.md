# ISSUE 1978 — Federal Court 2026 Term Backlog

## ISSUE
Malaysia's Federal Court reopens its post-recess sittings in May 2026 into a backlog crisis the new Chief Justice Wan Ahmad Farid Wan Salleh has named in writing. In his maiden Opening of the Legal Year speech on 12 January 2026, the CJ disclosed that criminal case registrations rose from 1,522,005 in 2021 to 2,486,567 by November 2025 — a 63.37% increase in four years — while civil cases rose 59.54%. He cited a single Johor Bahru High Court judge managing approximately 800 cases against a normal load of 200–250. On 27 April 2026 he issued a circular telling judges that court recess (1–12 June 2026) is not leave but a continuation of duty, and warned that "exit remains a dignified option" for judges who fail to deliver written judgments within the 8-week rule. Meanwhile, two of the apex court's most consequential recent decisions — Heidy Quah on CMA Section 233 (6 February 2026) and the seven prisoners detained at Ruler's pleasure (20 April 2026) — went against the rights-claimant.

## PERIOD
28 July 2025 (CJ Wan Ahmad Farid sworn in as 17th Chief Justice) — 10 May 2026. Forward-looking: 1–12 June 2026 court recess; full Legal Year 2026 ongoing.

## CONTEXT (Timeline)

- **28 July 2025**: Datuk Wan Ahmad Farid Wan Salleh sworn in as the 17th Chief Justice of Malaysia, succeeding Tun Tengku Maimun Tuan Mat. (Office of the Chief Registrar of the Federal Court, official portal; The Edge Malaysia)
- **August 2025**: In his early statements, CJ Wan Ahmad Farid commits to "protect judicial independence" and signals reform priorities including backlog reduction. (FMT, 6 Aug 2025)
- **27 November 2025**: CJ tells the public the judiciary is "examining alternative solutions to ease case backlog" — judicial commissioners to be appointed, mediation expanded, circuit courts revived. He cites the Johor Bahru example: a single judge handling approximately 800 cases vs. 200–250 normal. (FMT, 27 Nov 2025; The Star, 27 Nov 2025; BERNAMA)
- **6 December 2025**: "Justice comes closer to home" — Court of Appeal sits outside Putrajaya for the first time in decades. (The Star, 6 Dec 2025)
- **12 January 2026**: Opening of the Legal Year 2026 at MITEC, Kuala Lumpur. CJ Wan Ahmad Farid delivers his maiden OLY speech. **Discloses primary statistics**: criminal case registrations rose from 1,522,005 in 2021 to 2,486,567 by November 2025 (+63.37%); civil case registrations rose from 303,335 to 483,933 (+59.54%). Confirms 30% pay rise for High Court, Court of Appeal, and Federal Court judges — the first in a decade. Warns judges who fail to deliver written judgments on time that "the exit remains a dignified option" — paraphrased by Malay Mail as "kitchen is open, but the service must be timely." (Official CJ OLY 2026 speech PDF, kehakiman.gov.my; BERNAMA, 12 Jan 2026; Malay Mail, 12 Jan 2026; FMT, 12 Jan 2026; Malaysiakini, 12 Jan 2026; The Star, 12 Jan 2026)
- **6 February 2026**: Federal Court (Government of Malaysia v Heidy Quah Gaik Li) partly reverses a 2025 Court of Appeal landmark decision. Words "offensive" and "annoy" in CMA 1998 Section 233(1)(a), which the Court of Appeal had declared unconstitutional under Articles 8 and 10(1)(a) of the Federal Constitution, are reinstated as constitutional. The Federal Court holds the section can be "read down" to require "express intent to annoy another user or a body of users." (Federal Court press summary 6 Feb 2026, archived by ARTICLE 19; The Star 7 Feb 2026; FMT 6 Feb 2026; LHAG legal-analysis publication)
- **20 April 2026**: 5-member Federal Court bench chaired by CJ Wan Ahmad Farid Wan Salleh, Justice Collin Lawrence Sequerah delivering the unanimous verdict, denies leave to seven applicants who had been detained "at the pleasure of the Yang di-Pertuan Agong/Ruler" since they were minors under Section 97(2) of the Child Act 2001. They had sought to argue that their continued indefinite detention violated Articles 5(1) (right to life) and 8 (equality before the law) of the Federal Constitution following the 2023 abolition of mandatory death sentences and natural life imprisonment. The court rules their cases do not meet the "special and exceptional" threshold for leave; commutation via the pardon process remains the only avenue. Named applicants: N Harichandran, R Nomalan, Hafizul Hafiq Masri (all murder), Wong Soon Heng and Leong Soon Long (murder and kidnapping), Aiman Al-Rashid Yaacob (drug trafficking), Nguyen Doan Nhan (murder); aged 30–35, detained since childhood. (BERNAMA, 20 Apr 2026; FMT 20 Apr 2026; The Star 20 Apr 2026)
- **27 April 2026**: CJ issues circular ordering judges across all levels to stay on duty during the 1–12 June 2026 court recess. Registry offices at Federal Court, Court of Appeal, and High Courts remain open. Urgent matters handled by designated duty judges. "Court vacation is not a holiday." (Malay Mail 27 Apr 2026; The Star 27 Apr 2026)
- **28 April 2026**: Lawyers urge Parliament to address indefinite-detention provision for child offenders post-Federal Court ruling. (FMT 28 Apr 2026)
- **4 May 2026 (anchor date from radar signal)**: Federal Court term resumes after April-May inter-sitting break.

## RELEVANT LAW

1. **Federal Constitution of Malaysia**
   - **Article 5(1)** — "No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty save in accordance with law." Cited as constitutional basis by the 7 Ruler's-pleasure applicants.
   - **Article 8** — equality before the law; equal protection of the law. Same applicants and Heidy Quah CoA finding.
   - **Article 10(1)(a)** — freedom of speech and expression. Underlying CoA Heidy Quah finding 2025; reversed-on-this-point by FC 6 Feb 2026.
   - **Article 121** — judicial power vested in the High Courts and inferior courts; Federal Court of Malaysia, Court of Appeal as superior courts.
   - **Eighth Schedule** — state assembly essential provisions (cross-relevant to issue 1977 on Johor amendment).
2. **Communications and Multimedia Act 1998 (CMA)**
   - **Section 233(1)(a)** — knowingly makes/initiates communication that is "obscene, indecent, false, menacing or offensive in character with intent to annoy, abuse, threaten or harass another person." Words "offensive" and "annoy" central to Heidy Quah litigation.
3. **Child Act 2001**
   - **Section 97(2)** — detention "at the pleasure of the Yang di-Pertuan Agong / Ruler" for child offenders convicted of offences for which adults would receive death or natural life. Indefinite by design.
4. **Federal Constitution Article 42** — pardon and clemency power of the Yang di-Pertuan Agong and state Rulers. Cited by the FC 20 April 2026 as the remaining avenue for the 7 applicants.
5. **Rules of Court 2012** — 8-week rule for delivery of written grounds of judgment cited by the CJ as a key chokepoint litigants face.

## 12-DIMENSION RISK ASSESSMENT

1. **Sentiment** — MEDIUM: Topic is institutional. Risk of accumulating frustration toward the judiciary; we should be careful to attribute the crisis to systemic load growth, not to individual judges.
2. **Political** — LOW-MEDIUM: The CJ was appointed by the unity government; backlog is bipartisan/structural. No partisan framing is honest here.
3. **Ethnic** — LOW: No ethnic dimension to backlog or to the two illustrative cases.
4. **Religious** — LOW: Two referenced cases are not religious. We note the Federal Court has been the forum for several Shariah-civil jurisdiction matters (Iki Putra 2021, Nik Elin 2024) but those are not pending in this term.
5. **Narrative** — MEDIUM-HIGH: The framing risk is "two recent cases show a pattern of pro-state rulings." From a sample of two we cannot claim a pattern. We must report the two cases as factual events and let readers draw inferences, without editorializing the apex court as systemically pro-state.
6. **Completeness** — HIGH: Must include (a) the primary stats from the CJ's own speech (case-registration growth, 800-case Johor Bahru example, 30% pay rise), (b) the "no recess" April 2026 circular, (c) the two illustrative cases (Heidy Quah, 7 detainees), (d) the rights-access framing tied to Article 5(1)/8/10(1)(a), (e) the structural reform measures (judicial commissioners, mediation, circuit courts).
7. **Temporal** — HIGH: Story is anchored to the May 2026 term resumption but the underlying data spans 2021–2026. Frame as "as of 10 May 2026."
8. **Confidence** — HIGH on all CJ-speech-sourced statistics (primary source: official OLY 2026 PDF on kehakiman.gov.my). HIGH on the two case rulings (multiple-outlet convergence; BERNAMA + The Star + FMT). 9. **Sources** — HIGH: Primary sources include the official CJ OLY 2026 speech PDF (kehakiman.gov.my), official Federal Court press summary on Heidy Quah (archived by ARTICLE 19), and convergent reporting on the 7-detainees ruling. 11. **Economic** — LOW-MEDIUM: The 30% pay rise is fiscal; we report it as a CJ-framed integrity safeguard, not as scandal.

### 3R SPECIAL CAUTION (Royalty)
The Section 97(2) Child Act detention is "at the pleasure of the Yang di-Pertuan Agong/Ruler." We report this as the statutory mechanism and as cited by the Federal Court — Article 42 pardon process remains the only avenue. ## CONTRADICTIONS

- **800-cases stat — High Court vs Federal Court**: The 800 figure refers to a Johor Bahru *High Court* judge, not a Federal Court judge. Our copy must say "High Court" or "trial court" when citing this number; using it as a Federal Court statistic would overclaim. All four T4A coverage outlets (FMT, The Star, BERNAMA, Malay Mail) correctly attribute the 800 figure to the lower courts in the CJ's example.
- **Case-registration growth — what's the denominator**: 1,522,005 → 2,486,567 is *total* criminal registrations across all Malaysian courts (Magistrates, Sessions, High, Court of Appeal, Federal Court), not just the apex court. Same for the civil 60% growth. Our copy must say "case registrations across Malaysian courts" or "system-wide" — not "Federal Court case load."
- **Heidy Quah ruling — full reversal vs partial**: The Federal Court PARTLY reversed the Court of Appeal. The CoA had found Heidy Quah's specific prosecution unwarranted AND found CMA 233's "offensive"/"annoy" words unconstitutional. The FC affirmed the unwarranted-prosecution finding (Heidy Quah wins her individual case) but reversed the unconstitutionality finding (the words stay in the Act). Our copy must say "partly reversed" or "reversed on the constitutionality point" — saying "reversed" without qualification is inaccurate.
- **7-detainees ruling — leave denied, not merits**: The 20 April 2026 ruling denied LEAVE to bring the constitutional challenge — it did not decide the merits. The cases did not meet the "special and exceptional" threshold under the leave standard. Our copy must say "denied leave to challenge" or "refused leave to argue" — not "ruled the detention constitutional."
- **CJ's "exit" warning — to which judges**: The CJ's "dignified exit" warning was issued generally to all judges across all levels who fail to deliver written judgments on time, not specifically to Federal Court judges. Our copy must not imply the FC has been the worst offender.
