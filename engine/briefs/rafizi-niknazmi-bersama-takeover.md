# ISSUE 1993 — Two MPs resigned. No by-election. Constitution says it's fine.

## ISSUE
On Sunday 17 May 2026, at the PJ Performing Arts Centre before about 2,000 supporters, former Economy Minister Datuk Seri Rafizi Ramli and former Natural Resources & Environmental Sustainability Minister Nik Nazmi Nik Ahmad announced they would (i) quit PKR, (ii) vacate their Pandan and Setiawangsa parliamentary seats effective Monday 18 May 2026, and (iii) take over Parti Bersama Malaysia (PBM, also styled Malaysian United Party / MUP) — a dormant party registered with the Registrar of Societies in Penang on 2 December 2016 by founder Tan Gin Theam, who handed over the registration certificate on stage. Rafizi called it a "second kamikaze" mission, referring to his 2018 resignation from public office.

The headline picture mainstream coverage presents is a personality drama — ex-ministers ousted at the PKR elections found a new home in a shell party. The picture the public record actually supports is more structural: under Article 54(1) of the Federal Constitution, both seats can lawfully remain vacant until the next general election, because GE15 was held on 19 November 2022 and the 5-year parliamentary mandate expires on 18 November 2027 — meaning vacancies created less than two years before expiry trigger no by-election. PH deputy chairman and Transport Minister Anthony Loke confirmed this on 17 May 2026: "It has already passed the three year mark and even if asked to vacate, there is no necessity to have a by-election." Combined registered voters in Pandan and Setiawangsa — both Klang Valley seats — will therefore lack an elected MP for roughly 18 months unless the Speaker exercises discretion otherwise. The sequencing also appears engineered to side-step PKR's internal RM10 million anti-hopping bond, which has separately been litigated down to RM100,000 in the Court of Appeal (Zuraida Kamaruddin, Dec 2024).

## RELEVANT LAW

1. **Federal Constitution, Article 49A** — *Loss of Membership of House of Representatives on Party Hopping*. Inserted by the Constitution (Amendment) (No. 3) Act 2022, in force from 5 October 2022.
   - **49A(1)(a)**: A member loses their seat if they resign as a member of their political party.
   - **49A(1)(b)**: A member elected as an independent loses their seat if they join a political party.
   - **49A(2)(c)**: **Expulsion** from a party is **not** a trigger. (The "frog loophole".)
   - **A voluntary resignation of the seat under Article 51 is also outside Article 49A** — the seat is vacated by choice, not by operation of 49A. This is the mechanism Rafizi and Nik Nazmi are using.

2. **Federal Constitution, Article 51** — *Resignation of Members*. A member of the House of Representatives may resign their seat by writing under their hand addressed to the Speaker. This is the operative mechanism for the 18 May vacancy.

3. **Federal Constitution, Article 54(1)** — *Casual Vacancies*. Where a casual vacancy is established by the Election Commission, a by-election must be held within 60 days. **Provided that** where the vacancy occurs less than two years before the date on which Parliament's mandate expires under Article 55(3), no by-election is required and the seat remains vacant until the next general election. The threshold was amended from six months to two years (Malaysian Bar commentary on Article 54).

4. **Federal Constitution, Article 55(3)** — Parliament's life is five years from the first sitting unless dissolved earlier. The 13th Parliament first sat on 19 Dec 2022, giving a maximum mandate expiry around mid-December 2027. (Sources commonly cite an "18 November 2027" five-year-from-polling-day frame; the constitutional anchor is the first sitting.)

5. **PKR Constitution / Internal Undertaking** — Members agree to pay PKR **RM10 million** within seven days if they (a) resign from PKR, (b) join another political party, or (c) become an independent representative after winning on a PKR ticket. **Tested in court only once**: *PKR v Zuraida Kamaruddin*. High Court awarded the full RM10M (June 2023); Court of Appeal reduced it to RM100,000 (Dec 2024) on constitutional-right-to-association grounds; Federal Court has granted leave to appeal. Whether the bond is enforceable against an MP who **resigns the seat first** (the Rafizi/Nik Nazmi route) is **legally untested**.

6. **Societies Act 1966** — Governs the Registrar of Societies (RoS), which registered PBM in 2016 and would need to approve any major constitutional change at PBM (leadership transfer, name, structure). Per public statements, registration was already in place — Rafizi and Nik Nazmi are taking over an existing registered vehicle, not founding a new one.

## CONTRADICTIONS

- **"Three-year mark" (Loke) vs. "two-year window before mandate expiry" (Art. 54(1))**: Loke's phrasing is colloquial — he is referring to the fact that the 13th Parliament has passed its midpoint (Nov 2025) and is now in the final two-year window where Article 54(1) dispenses with by-elections. The constitutional anchor is **two years before the expiry of the 5-year mandate**, not "three years after GE." We will report the constitutional language and cite Loke for the political confirmation.
- **"Not party-hopping" (Rafizi/Nik Nazmi) vs. functional effect**: They argue the *legal* anti-hopping trigger (Article 49A) is not engaged because they vacate their seats first. This is technically correct. The functional effect on voters — winning on one party's ticket, leaving for another — is identical to party-hopping. We report both: the legal mechanics, and the gap between mechanics and electoral mandate.
- **PBM history**: Some coverage describes the takeover as Rafizi "forming a new party"; others correctly note PBM was registered in 2016 and is being repurposed. The Rakyat Post explicitly clarifies "Rafizi Ramli's New Party Is Old Bersama Malaysia." We will use *takeover of an existing dormant party*, not *founding*.
- **Parliament expiry date**: Some sources reference 18 November 2027 (five years from polling day, 19 Nov 2022); the constitutional anchor under Article 55(3) is five years from the first sitting (19 Dec 2022 → ~Dec 2027). The two-year window started on or near the corresponding 2025 anchor. We will report "approximately 18 months" until the latest possible GE16, not a precise day count.
- **Rafizi GE15 majority**: The Star reports 48,296. ElectionData.MY reports a slightly different figure (need to confirm). We will cite The Star (named, dated, primary-source-quality national daily). If we cannot confirm Setiawangsa majority to a primary source, we will report it as "approximately 12,000" rather than the specific 12,614.

## EDITORIAL ANGLE NOTES (for Stage 1 to draft against)

**Hook surface claim**: Two former ministers found a new party. New chapter for PKR rebels.

**Buried gap**: Two constituencies (Pandan + Setiawangsa, combined registered electorate well over a quarter of a million voters in the Klang Valley) will go without an elected MP for roughly 18 months. The Federal Constitution permits this because Article 54(1) was amended at some point to expand the no-by-election window from six months to two years before mandate expiry. Loke confirmed it openly. **The reframe candidates** (Stage 1 should pick one and not pad):
