# Research Brief — Issue 1989

**Slug:** `trump-xi-soybean-deal-vs-malaysia-palm-oil`
**Working headline:** Malaysia's Palm Oil to China Fell 39% Before Trump Met Xi
**Source date:** 2026-05-16
**Edition:** 1 (new)

## ISSUE

On 14–15 May 2026, US President Donald Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping held a two-day state-visit summit in Beijing. The lead-out from US officials — most prominently US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer (Bloomberg, 15 May) — was that China would commit to "**double-digit billion purchases of ags over the next three years, per year**" covering soybeans, corn, sorghum, milling wheat, beef and poultry, with 200 Boeing jets thrown in (Source [1], [2], [3]).

Mainstream commentary in Malaysia is already framing this as a direct threat to the country's palm oil sector, on the obvious mechanic: US soybeans → more crushed in China → more domestic Chinese soybean oil → less imported palm oil. That mechanic is real, but the framing is **two years late**.

Three pieces of context the headline hides:

1. **Malaysia's palm oil exports to China have already collapsed.** Plantation Minister Datuk Seri Johari Abdul Ghani told MPOC on the record that exports to China fell ~39% over the first ten months of 2025, and that the cause was **price, not geopolitics** — Malaysian CPO consistently above RM4,200/tonne, trading at one point at a USD120/tonne premium to soybean oil (Source [4]). The substitution shock the new Trump-Xi headline implies has largely already happened.

2. **China is not formally acknowledging the deal.** Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi said working teams are "still negotiating the relevant details" (Sources [5], [6]). Soybean traders responded by *selling* — July soybean futures fell more than 36 cents/bushel after Greer's remarks (Source [3]). The October 2025 prior commitment to import 25 million metric tonnes of US soybeans annually through 2028 is on paper, but China has zero new-crop export orders booked for MY 2025/26 (Source [7]).

3. **China's import diversification has already locked in Brazil.** In November 2025, China's state oilseed trader COFCO signed multi-billion agreements at the Shanghai International Import Expo to purchase ~20 million tonnes of Brazilian soybeans, soybean oil, palm oil and other agricultural products worth >US$10 billion, with ADM, Bunge, Cargill and Louis Dreyfus as counterparties (Sources [8], [9]). The Trump-Xi headline reverses a US-China trade dynamic that China has already engineered around.

The actual risks to Malaysian palm oil are structural and not new: Indonesian competition (Indonesia holds ~61% China market share, Malaysia ~27%), the EUDR coming into force December 2026, the eroding palm-oil-vs-soybean-oil price discount, and a 14.34% MoM drop in Malaysia's total palm oil exports in April 2026 (Source [10]). The Trump-Xi summit is at most a marginal accelerant on a story already two years in.

## CONTEXT (timeline with dates and sources)

- **November 2024:** Malaysia's revised CPO export-duty schedule takes effect: 7.0%–10% sliding scale based on reference price (RM3,151 → above RM4,050) (Source [11]).
- **1 January 2025:** Indonesia raises mandatory biodiesel blend from B35 to B40; allocates 15.6 billion litres of biodiesel — saves ~US$9.3B FX but costs US$2.1B in domestic subsidies; reduces Indonesian palm oil export surplus by ~2M tonnes (Sources [12], [13]).
- **Throughout 2025:** Malaysian CPO consistently trades above RM4,200/tonne; at one point soybean oil trades at a USD120/tonne discount to palm oil. China's soybean oil supply hits record 111.83 million tonnes; palm oil's substitution edge erodes (Sources [4], [14]).
- **February–October 2025:** Indonesian government confiscates 3.3 million hectares of palm land (~19% of national palm area), reducing Indonesia's 2025/26 production estimate to 46.7 million tonnes (Source [15]).
- **End-2024 full year:** Malaysia's palm oil exports to China: 1.39 million tonnes (-5.3% YoY); valued ~US$2.39 billion (Source [16]).
- **August 2025:** Malaysia's CPO export duty rises to 9% at reference price RM3,864.12/tonne (Source [11]).
- **September 2025:** EU delays EUDR implementation by 12 months — now Dec 30, 2026 for large operators; June 30, 2027 for micro/small enterprises (Source [17]).
- **24 October 2025 / ASEAN Summit, Kuala Lumpur:** Malaysia hosts ASEAN summit as 2025 chair. Anwar Ibrahim and Donald Trump sign a Reciprocal Trade Agreement; Chinese Premier Li Qiang also attends (Source [18], [19]).
- **November 2025:** Prior US-China commitment — China agrees to import 25 million metric tonnes of US soybeans annually through 2028 (Source [3], [7]).
- **November 2025:** COFCO signs ~20M tonnes / >US$10B Brazilian deal at Shanghai International Import Expo with ADM, Bunge, Cargill, Louis Dreyfus — covering soybeans, soybean oil, palm oil (Sources [8], [9]).
- **27 November 2025:** Plantation Minister Johari Abdul Ghani / MPOC: Malaysia's palm oil exports to China down ~39% in Jan–Oct 2025; cause is price, not geopolitics; CPO premium over soybean oil reached USD120/tonne at peak (Source [4]).
- **As at November 2025:** Malaysia's cumulative local-currency trade with China, Thailand and Indonesia reaches RM82.1 billion (vs RM3.6B in 2009); breakdown: RM62.6B China, RM10B Thailand, RM9.5B Indonesia (Sources [18], [20], [21]).
- **24 March 2026:** Anwar Ibrahim publicly highlights the RM82.1B local-currency trade figure; positions it as a hedge against US dollar reliance and SWIFT system risk (Sources [18], [21]).
- **April 2026:** Malaysia's palm oil exports drop 14.34% MoM to 1.30 million tonnes; stocks rise 1.71% to 2.30 million tonnes (Source [10]).
- **6 May 2026:** Bursa Malaysia CPO July contract drops 2.82% to RM4,577/tonne; bearish sentiment from global markets (Source [22]).
- **9 May 2026:** US Court of International Trade rules 2-1 against Trump's global 10% tariff (Source [23]).
- **11 May 2026:** Pre-summit — US Secretary of State Marco Rubio warns against "destabilising acts on Taiwan or anywhere in the Indo-Pacific" ahead of Trump's China visit (Source [24]).
- **14 May 2026 (Day 1, Beijing):** Trump-Xi summit opens. Xi warns Trump that differences over Taiwan could lead to a "clash". Bursa CPO July contract falls ~1.5% below RM4,450/tonne; market drivers include weak India demand (-26% MoM in April) and stronger ringgit (Sources [25], [26], [27]).
- **15 May 2026 (Day 2, Beijing):** USTR Greer (Bloomberg): China expected to commit to "double-digit billion purchases of ags over the next three years, per year" — soybeans plus "everything else" (Sources [1], [2]). Trump claims (Fox News): Xi agreed to soybeans, energy and 200 Boeing jets (vs 500 originally floated). Boeing shares -4%; July soybean futures drop >36 cents/bushel on lack of specifics (Source [28]). Chinese FM Wang Yi: working teams still negotiating details (Source [5]).

## RELEVANT LAW

- **Income Tax Act 1967, Schedule 3** — basis for CPO export duty calculation in Malaysia.
- **Palm Oil Refining Industry (Petroleum) Order** / **Customs Act 1967** — CPO export duty implementation; tiered 7–10% schedule per gazette.
- **Windfall Profit Levy Act 1998** — 3% levy on Peninsular Malaysia CPO above RM3,150/tonne and Sabah/Sarawak above RM3,650/tonne (post-Budget 2025 thresholds).
- **EU Regulation (EU) 2023/1115 (EUDR)** — Deforestation Regulation; large operators in scope from 30 Dec 2026, small/micro from 30 June 2027.
- **US Reciprocal Trade Agreement with Malaysia** (signed October 2025 at ASEAN KL summit) — terms govern Malaysian export access to US markets; full scope and review mechanism not publicly clarified per FMT/think-tank reporting.
- **Bank Negara Malaysia Act 2009, s. 23** — basis for BNM's bilateral Local Currency Settlement Framework arrangements.
- **WTO Agreement on Agriculture** — multilateral framework against which any US-China bilateral commitments would be tested for MFN consistency.
