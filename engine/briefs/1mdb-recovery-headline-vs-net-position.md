# Research Brief — Issue 1979

**Slug:** `1mdb-recovery-headline-vs-net-position`
**Working headline:** The RM31.3B 1MDB Recovery Headline Includes RM6B Still In Arbitration
**Source date:** 2026-05-12
**Edition:** 1 (new)

## ISSUE

On 6 May 2026, MACC Chief Commissioner Tan Sri Azam Baki announced that Malaysia had recovered RM31.3 billion of 1MDB-misappropriated funds as of end-2025 — "74.5% of the RM42 billion" lost, "among the world's highest" for a financial-crime case. The figure has been recycled across government and English-language media without three pieces of context:

1. **It includes a USD1.4 billion (≈RM6 billion) Goldman Sachs asset-recovery guarantee that is still in arbitration at the London Court of International Arbitration** — Goldman contends Malaysia has already recovered enough from third parties to satisfy the guarantee, and Malaysia disputes that. If the arbitrators rule for Goldman, the RM31.3B headline drops by about RM6B overnight.
2. **The RM42B denominator is total 1MDB-accumulated DEBT (mid-2015), not the US$4.5B (~RM21B) the US DOJ has separately established was actually misappropriated.** Conflating debt with theft makes "74.5%" arithmetically possible. Against the DOJ's US$4.5B baseline, recovery exceeds the proven theft — but that is a different sentence than "scandal almost recovered."
3. **Net of debt service, Malaysia is still in the hole.** As of March 2023 the Deputy Finance Minister stated the government had paid RM43.8 billion servicing 1MDB debt, with RM9.7 billion still outstanding. Gross-recovery talk obscures the net-fiscal position: more taxpayer money has gone out servicing 1MDB liabilities than has come back through recovery.

Recovery is also decelerating sharply. The bulk landed in 2020 (RM14.26B, Goldman cash settlement year). 2024 was RM716M; 2025 was RM1.57B. At the 2024-25 rate, even MACC's own forward target (RM7.9B remaining by 2027) would take about seven years, not two.

## CONTEXT (timeline with dates and sources)

- **2009–2015:** US DOJ later establishes ≥US$4.5B misappropriated from 1MDB by senior officials and associates (Source [1]).
- **Mid-2015:** 1MDB accumulated debts of RM42B (~US$12B at the time); S&P/Fitch downgrade 1MDB bonds to junk (Source [2]).
- **2016:** US DOJ launches landmark Kleptocracy Initiative civil-forfeiture campaign (41 actions, US$1.7B seized) (Source [1]).
- **24 July 2020:** Malaysia and Goldman Sachs announce US$3.9B settlement: US$2.5B cash + US$1.4B asset-recovery guarantee (Sources [3], [4]).
- **6 August 2021:** US DOJ confirms over US$1B repatriated to Malaysia (Source [5]).
- **October 2023:** Goldman Sachs files arbitration at the London Court of International Arbitration over the asset-recovery guarantee, arguing Malaysia has already recovered the guaranteed amount from other parties (Sources [6], [7]).
- **13 June 2024:** US DOJ announces total of US$1.4B repatriated to Malaysia under the Kleptocracy Initiative — the largest in the program's history (Source [8]).
- **14 February 2025:** Finance Minister II Datuk Seri Amir Hamzah Azizan / Treasury Secretary-General Datuk Johari Abdul Ghani confirms Goldman asset-guarantee dispute still in arbitration (Source [9]).
- **14 October 2025:** Ministry of Finance press statement: RM20.73B recovered from financial institutions — Goldman (≈RM16.5B), AmBank (RM2.83B), JPMorgan Switzerland (RM1.4B) (Sources [10], [11]).
- **6 May 2026:** MACC Chief Commissioner Azam Baki announces cumulative recovery of RM31.3B (74.5% of RM42B), with yearly breakdown 2019–2025; targets US$2B more by end-2027 (Sources [12], [13], [14], [15]).
- **7 May 2026:** The Star headline reframes the same announcement as "Graft buster targets RM7.9bil 1MDB recovery by 2027" — the remaining target, not the cumulative claim (Source [16]).
