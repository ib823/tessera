## ISSUE

Mainstream Malaysian political discourse routinely frames Malay grievance as inter-ethnic — Malays losing economic ground to other races. T4A's accumulated record across ~14 audited issues tells a different structural story: the largest leaks in Bumiputera redistribution are intra-ethnic and institutional. Programmes built to lift B40 Malays mostly reach middlemen, absentee board appointees, and T20 Bumiputera households. The grievance is real and the data supports it — but it is aimed at the wrong target when framed as a racial competition.

## ACTORS

- **Bumiputera contractors** (genuine SMEs) — the intended beneficiaries of the 30% procurement quota. Lose RM12B/year to commission-seekers per issue 1170.
- **Ali Baba middlemen** — Bumiputera-quota holders who sell contracts to non-Bumi operators for 5-15% commissions. 34% of surveyed contractors admit participation per MACC 2025 study (issue 1170).
- **Political GLC appointees** — 342 individuals across 48 GLCs and statutory bodies earning RM680M combined in 2025 (issue 1227).
- **Bumiputera property buyers** — receive 5-15% discount with no means-testing; same discount applies to T20 and B40 households (issue 1625).
- **Non-Bumiputera property buyers** — absorb the cross-subsidy through ~RM4.8B in higher prices in 2024 (issue 1625).
- **MACC** — investigative body; prosecuted 42 Ali Baba cases in 2025 against an estimated 8,000-12,000 arrangements (issue 1170). Total CPI-linked enforcement: 342 prosecutions out of 8,400 reports = 4% rate (issue 1208).
- **PKB (Perbadanan Pembangunan Kampong Bharu)** — set up 2012, has not delivered the 121-hectare master redevelopment after 40 years of plans (issue 1229).

## RELEVANT LAW / POLICY

- **New Economic Policy (NEP, 1971)** and subsequent NDP/NTP — established the Bumiputera redistribution framework. Originally time-limited; functionally permanent.
- **Government procurement Bumiputera quota** — 30% of federal procurement value reserved for Bumiputera-owned businesses. Source: Ministry of Finance procurement guidelines, referenced in issue 1170.
- **State Bumiputera housing discount mandate** — 5-15% mandatory discount on new property launches, varies by state. Required by all state governments per issue 1625.
- **Malay Reserve Land** (Malay Reservations Enactment 1933 and state equivalents) — land restriction relevant to Kampung Baru paralysis (issue 1229).
- **Malaysian Qualifications Framework (MQF) 2007** — establishes SKM-degree equivalence; not honoured in employer practice (issue 1401).
