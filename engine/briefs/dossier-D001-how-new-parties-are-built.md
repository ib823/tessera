## ISSUE
The recurring Malaysian political move of forming a new political vehicle 6–18 months before a national or state election, and the structural conditions that make it rational, repeatable, and overwhelmingly aimed at *pivotal-player status* rather than majority government. Voter-facing explainer with full primary-source citation.

## PERIOD
1951 (IMP) through 2024, with forward-looking notes on the 2026–27 cycle.

## CONTEXT — TIMELINE
- **1951** — IMP founded by Onn Jaafar after UMNO split [HIGH: National Archives].
- **1957** — Federal Constitution promulgated; original Article 113 caps malapportionment at 15% [HIGH: Federal Constitution 1957 original text].
- **1962** — Constitutional amendment removes the 15% malapportionment cap [HIGH: Constitution (Amendment) Act 1962].
- **1969** — May 13 emergency; suspension of Parliament under Article 150 [HIGH: National Operations Council report; Article 150 text].
- **1974** — Petroleum Development Act vests petroleum in PETRONAS; 5% royalty to producing states [HIGH: PDA 1974].
- **1987** — Operasi Lalang; 106 detentions under ISA; three newspapers closed [HIGH: Suhakam records; AI Malaysia briefing 1987].
- **1988** — Semangat 46 founded by Tengku Razaleigh after UMNO Team A / Team B fight [HIGH: ROS gazette].
- **1990** — GE8: Semangat 46 wins 8 seats in alliance with PAS and DAP [HIGH: EC results].
- **1999** — KeADILan founded after Anwar's sacking and Reformasi; wins 5 seats in GE10 [HIGH: EC results].
- **2008** — GE12: BN loses two-thirds majority for first time [HIGH: EC results].
- **2014** — Royal Commission of Inquiry on Sabah Illegal Immigrants reports [HIGH: RCI report 2014].
- **2016** — Bersatu founded by Mahathir and Muhyiddin from UMNO splinter [HIGH: ROS gazette].
- **2018** — Sarawak State Sales Tax (Amendment) Ordinance imposes 5% sales tax on petroleum products [HIGH: Sarawak Sales Tax Ordinance amendment 2018].
- **2018** — GE14: PH coalition defeats BN; first peaceful transfer of federal power [HIGH: EC results].
- **2020** — Sheraton Move: Bersatu MPs withdraw from PH; Muhyiddin becomes PM under Article 40 discretion [HIGH: parliamentary record].
- **2020** — GRS (Gabungan Rakyat Sabah) formed weeks before PRN16; wins state [HIGH: ROS gazette; Sabah EC results].
- **2021** — UNDI18 effective; automatic voter registration begins [HIGH: Election Commission notification].
- **2021** — Constitutional amendment restoring Sabah and Sarawak to equal-partner status in MA63 framing [HIGH: Constitution (Amendment) Act 2021].
- **2022** — Constitution (Amendment) (No. 3) Act 2022 inserts Article 49A — anti-hopping law [HIGH: Constitutional amendment text].
- **2022** — GE15: hung parliament; PH 82, PN 74, BN 30 of 222 seats; Anwar appointed PM after Article 40 discretion by the Agong [HIGH: EC results; Istana Negara statement].

## KEY STATISTICS

### Malapportionment
- Original 1957 constitutional cap: **15%** deviation [HIGH: 1957 Constitution].
- Cap removed: **1962** [HIGH: Constitution (Amendment) Act 1962].
- Largest-to-smallest parliamentary voter ratio (most recent delineation 2018): **approximately 9–10:1** [HIGH: derived from EC voter roll; published by Bersih and Wong Chin Huat].
- Approximate Bangi voters: 178,000+ [HIGH: 2018 delineation roll; verify with current EC roll].
- Approximate Putrajaya voters: 27,000–30,000 [HIGH: 2018 delineation roll; verify].

### Money
- Election deposit: **RM10,000** federal parliamentary, **RM5,000** state assembly [HIGH: Election Offences Act].
- Deposit forfeit threshold: **less than 1/8 (12.5%) of votes** [HIGH: Election Offences Act].
- Total deposits for full 222-seat federal contest: **RM2.22 million** [HIGH: arithmetic from above].
- Legal campaign expenditure cap per parliamentary candidate: **RM200,000** [HIGH: Election Offences Act Section 19].
- Estimated real campaign spend per competitive peninsular parliamentary seat: **RM1m–RM3m** [MED: Gomez; Bersih estimates; verify].
- Estimated real campaign spend per Sabah/Sarawak federal seat: **RM3m–RM8m+** [MED: regional reporting; verify].

### Sabah / Sarawak fiscal
- Sarawak State Sales Tax on petroleum products: **5%** since 2018 [HIGH: Sales Tax Ordinance].
- Estimated annual revenue from Sarawak SST: **RM2.7bn–RM4bn**, oil-price-dependent [MED: Sarawak state budget statements; verify current year].
- Federal seats held by Sabah + Sarawak: **56 of 222 (25.2%)** [HIGH: EC seat allocation].

### Felda
- Felda original settler households: **~112,000** across **317 schemes** [HIGH: Felda official records; verify].
- Second-generation Felda population estimate: **~1 million** [MED: Felda statements; academic estimates; verify].

### Civil service

- As a share of working-age population: **roughly 1 in 19** [LOW: arithmetic from civil-service total and DOSM working-age estimate; verify].

## CONTRADICTIONS

1. **Number of original Felda settlers.** Felda's own corporate communications variously cite figures from 94,000 to 122,000 depending on cutoff date. Dossier uses **~112,000** as the commonly cited steady-state number; flag for re-verification.

2. **Civil service total.** PSD figures over the past five years range from 1.5m to 1.7m depending on inclusion of contract workers and statutory bodies. Dossier uses **~1.6m**; flag.

3. **Sarawak SST revenue.** State budget statements 2019–2024 vary widely. The dossier uses a **RM2.7bn–RM4bn** range to honour the variance; do not collapse to a single number.

4. **Sabah-Sarawak federal seat count.** Some sources include or exclude Labuan FT (1 seat). Dossier uses **56 of 222 (25.2%)** including Labuan; flag.

5. **Real campaign spend per seat.** Estimates vary by an order of magnitude. Dossier presents as a range and attributes to Gomez and Bersih commentary rather than a single hard number.

6. **GRS founding date.** Some sources date GRS to July 2020 (initial registration), others to September 2020 (umbrella formalisation). Dossier uses **"weeks before PRN16"** to honour both.
